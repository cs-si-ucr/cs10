Introduction

A loop that appears in the body of another loop is often called a nested loop.

The loops on the inside of a nested loop construct are called inner loops while the loop on the outside of a nested loop construct is called the outer loop

Building a complex program by making small testable additions and changes is called incremental programming

A loop construct that executes the body of the loop before checking the loop condition is called a do-while loop
